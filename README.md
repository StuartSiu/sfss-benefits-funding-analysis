# A Comparison of Various Funding Arrangements for SFSS' Group Benefit Plan

Course: Risk 360W Spring2025, Simon Fraser University

Team Members: Stuart Siu, Terry Choi, Theresia Verena

## Confidentiality Notice: 
Any data, calculations and final reports associated with this project are subject to a strict Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) regarding the SFSS Health and Dental Plan 2022-2024 Claims and Renewal reports. 
To respect data privacy and client confidentiality, no proprietary data, analysis, or results are hosted in this repository.

## Objective
Conducted a comprehensive scenario testing analysis across various insurance funding models to project reserve requirements and forecast 3-year plan costs for a university group health and dental benefit plan.

## The Business Problem
Group benefit plans must carefully balance premium affordability with the necessity of maintaining adequate reserves to cover unexpected claims utilization. The project required analyzing historical claims data to determine the optimal funding arrangement: 
1. fully-insured,
2. self-funded / administrative-service-only (ASO),
3. rate-stabilization-fund (RSF),

## Actuarial Methodology
While the underlying claims data is confidential, the analysis was executed using the following standard actuarial frameworks:
- Experience Rating: Analyzed historical claims utilization and demographic shifts to reliably project plan premiums.
- Trend Analysis: Applied linear trending to forecast healthcare and dental cost inflation over a 3-year projection period.
- Scenario Testing: Modeled multiple funding arrangements to stress-test required reserve levels under various high risk scenarios.
